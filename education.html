Education

TRUEX is neutral. This page is here to help you interpret records correctly — and avoid being misled by small samples, selective reporting, or marketing.

Core idea: A betting record means nothing unless it is complete, time-locked, and cannot be edited. TRUEX exists to make “proof” normal.

ROI vs Win Rate

Win rate alone doesn’t tell you if someone is profitable. Odds matter. A bettor can win 60% of heavy favorites and still lose money, or win 48% of plus money and be profitable.

Rule of thumb: If you don’t know the odds distribution, win rate is incomplete information.

Why sample size matters

Short streaks happen. A 20–50 bet run can look amazing by pure variance. Real edges show up over time, across different market conditions.

  • Small samples: easy to fake, easy to misunderstand
  • Large samples: harder to fake, more meaningful

Drawdowns and variance

Even profitable bettors experience drawdowns. A record without drawdowns is often incomplete or filtered.

Max Drawdown helps you understand risk. Two bettors can have the same ROI and wildly different drawdowns.

Beating the close (CLV)

Closing Line Value (CLV) is a signal — not a guarantee — that a bettor is consistently getting good numbers. Over time, bettors who beat the close tend to perform better than bettors who don’t.

  • CLV positive: often indicates good timing/price
  • CLV negative: may indicate chasing / poor entry
Important: CLV is most meaningful in liquid markets with reliable closing lines. It can be noisy in small or illiquid markets.

Timing and odds movement

Many “good records” are driven by timing — not just picks. If someone posts picks after the move, you’re not getting the same bet.

TRUEX focuses on lock-time enforcement and immutable logging to prevent “after the fact” reporting.

Common ways records get misrepresented

  • Deleting losses
  • Only posting winners
  • Backfilling old bets
  • Changing odds after the fact
  • Claiming “I got -110” when market was -130

  • Lock-time enforced: no post-start submissions
