Meanings — Canonical Definitions (LOCKED v1)
This page defines terms exactly as TRUEX uses them.
These definitions are authoritative. They are not flexible, motivational, or outcome-based.

Integrity Verdict (PASS / WARN / FAIL)

Definition: A classification of observed market interaction quality over time, based on pricing behavior, slippage, and consistency.

Does not mean: That a strategy is good, bad, profitable, or unprofitable.

Process Integrity

Definition: The stability and quality of how bets interact with market prices across time, market class, and execution windows.

Does not mean: Prediction accuracy or outcome success.

Outcome Masking

Definition: Wins occurring despite deteriorating price quality or execution conditions.

Does not mean: That the underlying process is sound or improving.

CLV (Closing Line Value)

Definition: The difference between the price at which a bet was placed and the market’s closing price.

Does not mean: Guaranteed edge, profit, or correctness of a bet.

Slippage

Definition: Bets being placed at prices worse than the eventual market close.

Does not mean: A single bad execution or mistake.

Consistency

Definition: The degree to which execution behavior remains stable across time, market classes, and bet selection patterns.

Does not mean: Repeating the same outcomes.

Evidence vs Progress

Definition: Progress indicates early feedback before sufficient sample thresholds are met. Evidence indicates thresholds have been satisfied.

Does not mean: That early results are reliable or actionable.

Operational

Definition: A state in which minimum data thresholds have been met for evaluation.

Does not mean: Approval, endorsement, or readiness to scale.

Fractured

Definition: Execution behavior that varies meaningfully across timing, market class, or selection patterns.

Does not mean: That any single bet or sport is invalid.

Liquid Props vs Thin Props

Definition: Liquid props are standard, high-liquidity markets such as full-game over/unders and widely traded player totals. Thin props include novelty or low-liquidity markets.

Does not mean: That thin props are hidden or ignored in the UI.

Combined Execution

Definition: The interaction of multiple models or bet sources evaluated as a single execution process.

Does not mean: That models are averaged, optimized, or ranked.

Scoped View

Definition: A filtered view limited to a single model or subset for inspection.

Does not mean: A standalone verdict override.

TRUEX does not provide advice. It does not compute EV. It does not tell you what to do.
