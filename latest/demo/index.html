What you’re looking at

The exhibits below describe three observable patterns:
  • CLV decay (prices getting worse relative to close)
  • Slippage clustering (more bets placed at worse-than-close prices)
  • Outcome masking (wins occurring inside negative pricing regimes)
No EV. No picks. No “fix this.” Only observation.

Exhibit A — Outcome masking

In this example, outcomes include wins across multiple CLV buckets — including buckets with negative pricing. That can create a false sense of stability even while pricing quality deteriorates.
Map to Charts → “Outcomes vs Price Quality”: wins exist even where CLV is negative.

Exhibit B — CLV decays over time

CLV trends downward across the sample. The zero-line is visible; points increasingly fall below it. That is a market-interaction signal, independent of wins/losses.
Map to Charts → “CLV vs Time”: later points cluster below 0.

Exhibit C — Slippage rate rises

Slippage frequency increases across windows, meaning a larger share of bets are placed at prices worse than the close. This can happen through timing drift, market class mixing, or execution inconsistency.
Map to Charts → “Slippage Frequency Over Time”: the line rises across windows.

Plain-English interpretation

The combined execution process is interacting with the market at worse prices more frequently over time. Outcomes include wins, but the pricing trend suggests those wins may be masking deterioration. The verdict applies to the combined process, not any single bet, sport, or model in isolation.
TRUEX does not provide recommendations. It does not compute EV. It does not tell you what to do.
