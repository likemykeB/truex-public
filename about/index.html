Copy & Education — What to expect (LOCKED v1)
TRUEX explains truth. It does not persuade, optimize, or gamify.

A) What TRUEX Measures

TRUEX measures process integrity — how bets interact with markets over time — not outcomes, not predictions, and not “edge claims.” The output is a verdict about pricing interaction and execution consistency under observed conditions.

B) Consistency Expectation

TRUEX works best when the same execution logic is applied consistently. If execution behavior changes across timing, market class, or bet selection patterns, interpretability declines and inconsistency flags may appear.
TRUEX does not identify strategies.

C) Progress vs Evidence

Progress reports are early feedback and may change. Evidence status indicates whether sample thresholds are met. Early feedback ≠ proof.

D) Props Disclosure

Liquid props may be included in integrity calculations. Thin props are excluded from integrity calculations but may still be visible for transparency.
  • PROP_LIQUID → included in integrity math
  • PROP_THIN → excluded from integrity math (still visible)

E) Multi-Model Warning

Combined views evaluate the interaction of multiple models as a single execution process. Mixing models can reduce interpretability. Model pages are scoped views; “All Models” reflects combined execution.

What TRUEX Does Not Do

  • Compute EV
  • Recommend bets
  • Provide strategy advice
  • Show profit curves or ROI
  • Optimize execution or sizing
